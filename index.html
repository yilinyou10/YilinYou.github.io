About

My research lies at the intersection of building physics, smart glazing materials, façade engineering, and urban building energy modelling. I am particularly interested in the development and performance evaluation of thermotropic and thermochromic smart window systems that can dynamically regulate solar heat gain, daylight availability, and indoor thermal conditions.

My work combines experimental measurements, EnergyPlus simulation, climate data processing, and city-scale building energy assessment. The long-term goal of my research is to support the design of climate-adaptive building envelopes and low-carbon urban environments, especially under increasingly hot and uncertain future climate conditions.

I am currently visiting in NUS and involved in research related to smart windows, high-performance façades, tropical and humid climates, and future-climate-driven urban building energy performance.

Research Interests

Smart Windows

Thermotropic glazing, thermochromic materials, hydrogel-based smart windows, solar modulation, and adaptive transparent envelopes.

Building Energy Simulation

EnergyPlus modelling, envelope performance evaluation, heating and cooling energy analysis, and parametric simulation workflows.

Daylighting & Comfort

Useful daylight illuminance, glare-related performance, indoor thermal environment, visual comfort, and integrated building performance.

Urban Building Energy Modelling

GIS-based urban building energy modelling, city-scale retrofit assessment, building stock analysis, and future climate scenarios.
